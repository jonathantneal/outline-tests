Types of tequila

There are different types of tequila. Each is made in a different way, often with different ingredients, and each has its own distinct characteristics.

Blanco tequila

Blanco tequila (sometimes known as White Platinum tequila) is the purest form of the Blue Agave spirit. It is clear and unaged, and can be bottled immediately after distillation.

Joven tequila

Joven tequila (sometimes called Gold tequila), is a "mixto". This means that flavouring and colourants are added to it before it's bottled, making it a less pure and therefore less expensive form of the drink.

Pure Gold tequila

Gold tequila is sometimes created by blending Blanco and Reposado tequilas. When this is the case, the tequila keeps its 100% pure agave classification.

Reposado tequila

Reposado tequila is aged in wooden barrels for between two and eleven months. This gives the tequila a smooth texture, and gives it a golden hue in the bottle.
